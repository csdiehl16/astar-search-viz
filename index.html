How it works

Imagine you are dropped in a random city, with a few roads leading out of it. Each road has a sign showing how far it is to the next town. You know what city you want to end up in, and approximately where it is on a map — but you don't know anything about the road network in between.

A* uses a combination of how expensive it is to reach a node so far (g(n)) and a heuristic, an educated guess of how far that node still is from the goal (h(n)) — here, straight-line (Euclidean) distance.

f(n) = g(n) + h(n)

The search keeps a priority queue of cities to check next, ordered by the lowest f(n). We pop the top item, expand all roads leading out of it onto the frontier, and repeat until we reach the goal.

The Python implementation is based on the public examples from "AI: A Modern Approach".
